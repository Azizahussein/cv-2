Stress Response and Adaptation: An fMRI and AI

Explored the human stress response and adaptation using fMRI and AI language models (LLMs) to analyze both brain activity and cognitive reactions to stress. Through fMRI, we observed brain areas like the amygdala and prefrontal cortex involved in stress regulation, tracking how individuals respond to stressors. Simultaneously, AI LLMs were used to simulate stress-related language patterns and generate responses to stress scenarios. We compared neural responses with AI-generated reactions to explore how well AI mirrors human stress adaptation, aiming to deepen our understanding of both human emotional regulation and AIs potential to simulate similar processes.
